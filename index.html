Lights Out Game

Click on the squares to toggle their state. Turn off all the lights to win!

Addendum: Development Process and Creative Choices

Process & Creative Choices:

  • Game Setup: I started by creating the basic structure for the Lights Out game. I chose a 5x5 grid for a balanced level of challenge and manageability.
  • Random Configuration: The board starts white, and random "clicks" are simulated to toggle squares, ensuring the puzzle is solvable upon loading.
  • CSS Styling: The styling focuses on clear visibility of the grid. Squares toggle between black and white when clicked, using smooth transitions.
  • Game Logic: A 2D array is used to track the state of each square. Clicking a square toggles its state and that of its neighboring squares. The game ends when all squares are off.
  • Challenges: One challenge was ensuring the board is always solvable. By simulating clicks on a solved board, I ensure that the game is always winnable.
  • Insights: The process highlighted the importance of structuring game logic effectively. It also demonstrated how to manage event-driven behavior and ensure smooth user interaction.
